Saw this piece on [The Economist](https://www.economist.com/science-and-technology/2020/05/30/flying-people-to-the-space-station-is-spacexs-biggest-deal-yet) that discusses why SpaceX can fly people to space at less than 10% of what it used to cost NASA. One of the key reasons is a shift from what is essentially a time and materials contract to a fixed-price contract. Powerful message on the importance of contract design. (fixed price contracts are not always better - [paper](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2017.2993) shows when it does substantially worse.)
