Saw this piece on [The economist](https://www.economist.com/science-and-technology/2020/05/30/flying-people-to-the-space-station-is-spacexs-biggest-deal-yet) that discusses why SpaceX can fly people to space at less than 10% of what it used to cost NASA. One of the key reasons is a shift from what is essentially a time & materials contract to a fixed-price contract. Contract design matters! (Evidence from my [paper](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2017.2993) on when time & materials contract do better.)
