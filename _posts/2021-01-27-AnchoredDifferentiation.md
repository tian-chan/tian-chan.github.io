Comparative processes are crucial to how consumers evaluate design. What we show, however, is that consumers can be sophisticated in such comparisons. Given a novel design, they would lean on its similarities to past designs to understand its functionalities, but also lean on dis-similarities to other contemporary designs to seek distinctiveness and express their individuality. The accepted manuscript can be found [here](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/348807344_Anchored_Differentiation_The_Role_of_Temporal_Distance_in_the_Comparison_and_Evaluation_of_New_Product_Designs).
